What are we doing?

EUMs is the abbreviation for Supply End User Monitoring System. EUMs provides feedback about the quality, impact and efficiency of the supply component of country programmes. It is a regular and systematic checking of the supplies received by the end users against the targeted quantity given in the planning documents. It enables improvements in both supply planning and procurement.

Polio and Global Polio Eradication Initiative

Polio is a highly infectious disease with no cure. It mainly affects children who are younger than five years old. The virus of polio can cause paralysis by destroying the nervous system. The Global Polio Eradication Initiative (GPEI) was launched in 1988, by WHO, Rotary International, CDC and UNICEF. By immunising children with effective vaccines, more than 99 percent of polio cases have been reduced by the immunisation; more than 10 million children have been saved from being paralysed.

What are we doing?

Today, polio remains endemic in Nigeria, Pakistan, and Afghanistan. To eradicate polio worldwide, UNICEF is building Rhizome, a real-time data platform to be used by GPEI employees from all levels. Rhizome platform collects data from lowest possible level by allowing users to enter data via web form. After data has been aggregated, Rhizome platform can visualise the data for district, country and leadership level managers. With Rhizome, UNICEF have more comprehensive access to immunisation campaign related data. Rhizome is also a open source software. The future version of Rhizome will be suitable for any other similar data.
